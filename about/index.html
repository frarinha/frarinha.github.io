About

Currently working as a freelance videographer. Stories are how we learn, communicate and remember. I have always been fascinated by the power of moving pictures, and I’m grateful for being able to make a living by sharing my creations. Although making films is one of my biggest passions, I think it would suit better as a hobby. At the moment, I’m seeking to transition to the InfoSec field.

I’m looking for a Penetration Testing position.

I have been around computers since I can remember. My first computer ran windows 98 and I mostly used it to play games and write gibberish in text editors. Pressing keys on a keyboard and seeing symbols appear on a screen felt like magic at the time. This quickly turned into an interest in video games, that were a big part of my childhood.

Although naturally creative, I’m also a very curious person. At the time, I thought that science was the best way to find answers to understand the world. So, in my academic journey I pursued sciences and technologies. In university I learned about computer systems, programming and networks. Despite the fact that I have a good understanding of how computers work, it still feels like magic to me.

Hacking is about finding a way to outsmart a system using it’s own features to our advantage. There’s nothing more fun than that. Funny enough, hacking computer systems also meets my creative side. I find hacking very similar to art. Capture the flag challenges are a big part of my learning methodology because I’m a very hands-on learner. Basically they’re puzzles that teach you how to break into computer systems.
